\question[5]

Consider the following language \(L\) over \(\{a, b, c, d\}\):
  \begin{equation*}
    \{a^l b^m c^n d^{l + m + n} \in \{a, b, c, d\}^* \mid l, m, n \ge 0 \}
  \end{equation*}
  %
  \begin{parts}
    \part \withpoints{3} Produce a context-free grammar \(G\) such that \(L(G) = L\), i.e. that
    \(G\) generates the language \(L\), and show a derivation of the word
    \(ab^2cd^4\) in \(G\). 
    %
    \part \withpoints{2} Prove that \(L\) is not a regular language.
  \end{parts}
  %
  \SetTotalWidth
  \begin{solutionorbox}[\fill]
    \withpoints{2} Context-free grammar:
    \begin{align*}
      A & ::= aAd \mid B        \\
      B & ::= bBd \mid C        \\
      C & ::= cCd \mid \epsilon
    \end{align*}

    %
    \withpoints{1} Derivation of \(ab^2cd^4 = abbcdddd\):
    \begin{align*}
      A & \to aAd \\
        & \to aBd \\
        & \to abBdd \\
        & \to abbBddd \\
        & \to abbCddd \\
        & \to abbcCdddd \\
        & \to abbcdddd \\
        & = ab^2cd^4
    \end{align*}
    A correct \emph{parse tree} will be accepted as well.
    %

    \withpoints{2} We prove that \(L\) is not a regular language by using the
    pumping lemma. To the contrary, assume that \(L\) is regular. Then, by the
    pumping lemma, there exists a pumping length \(p\) such that any word \(w
    \in L\) with \(|w| \ge p\) can be decomposed into three parts \(w = xyz\)
    such that:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \(|xy| \le p\)
      \item \(|y| > 0\)
      \item \(xy^iz \in L\) for all \(i \ge 0\)
    \end{itemize}

    Choose a candidate word \(w = a^pb^pd^{2p}\). Then, we can decompose \(w\)
    as \(w = xyz\) with the conditions as above. Since \(|xy| \le p\), we know
    that \(xy\) must be in the first part of the word, and thus \(x = a^n\) and
    \(y = a^m\) for some \(n \ge 0, m > 0, n + m \le p\). However, \(xy^iz =
    a^{p + m\cdot(i - 1)}b^pd^{2p}\) is not in \(L\) for any \(i \not = 1\).
    Thus, \(w\) cannot be pumped, and we have a contradiction.

    Therefore, \(L\) is not a regular language.
  \end{solutionorbox}
